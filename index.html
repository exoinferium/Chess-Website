Chess Rules

Objective

Checkmate your opponent's king - put it under attack so it cannot escape. The game is won when checkmate occurs.

Starting Position

White pieces start at rows 1-2, black pieces at rows 7-8. Each player has 16 pieces: 1 king, 1 queen, 2 rooks, 2 bishops, 2 knights, and 8 pawns.

Turn Order

White always moves first. Players alternate turns moving one piece at a time. You cannot move into check.

Check & Checkmate

Check: King is under attack. Checkmate: King is in check and has no legal moves. The game ends in checkmate.

Special Moves

Castling: King and rook move simultaneously under certain conditions. En Passant: Pawn captures opponent's pawn that just moved two squares.

Draw Conditions

Stalemate: King not in check but no legal moves. Threefold repetition, fifty-move rule, or insufficient material also result in draws.

Chess Pieces & Movement

♔

King

Movement: One square in any direction (up, down, left, right, diagonal)

Value: Infinite (cannot be sacrificed)

♕

Queen

Movement: Any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally

Value: 9 points (most powerful piece)

♖

Rook

Movement: Any number of squares horizontally or vertically

Value: 5 points

♗

Bishop

Movement: Any number of squares diagonally

Value: 3 points

♘

Knight

Movement: L-shaped move (2 squares one way, 1 square perpendicular). Can jump over pieces!

Value: 3 points

♙

Pawn

Movement: Forward 1 square (2 on first move). Captures diagonally forward. Can promote to any piece when reaching the end!

Value: 1 point
